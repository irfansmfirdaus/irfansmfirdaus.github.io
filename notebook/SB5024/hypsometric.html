Hypsometric Equation

The hypsometric equation is expressed:

$$ Z_1 - Z_2 = \overline{H}\ ln(\frac{p_1}{p_2}) = \frac{R_d \overline{T_v}}{g_0}\ ln(\frac{p_1}{p_2}) $$

Example:

Compute the thickness of layer $850 mb - 750 mb$

Pressure (mb) Temperature ($\unicode{xb0}C$) Specific Humidity (g/kg)
850 10.4 9.2
700 1.8 3.5

From table, we can calculate $T_v$ for each layer.

$$T_v = (1 + 0.61q)T$$

  • $850\;mb$

    $q = 9.2 \; g/kg = 0.0092$

    $T = 10.4 \, \unicode{xb0}C = 283.55 \,K$

    $$\begin{aligned} T_v &= (1 + 0.61(0.0092))\,283.55 \\ &= 285.14 \,K \end{aligned}$$

  • $700 mb$

    $q = 3.5 \; g/kg = 0.0035$

    $T = 1.8 \, \unicode{xb0}C = 274.95 \,K$

    $$\begin{aligned} T_v &= (1 + 0.61(0.0035))\,274.95 \\ &= 275.53 \,K \end{aligned}$$

From equation:

$$\begin{aligned} Z_1 - Z_2 &= \frac{R_d \overline{T_v}}{g_0}\ ln(\frac{p_1}{p_2}) \\ &= -[\frac{287(\frac{285.14+275.53}{2})}{9.81}] \, ln(\frac{700}{800}) \\ &= 1592.36 \, m \end{aligned}$$

So, the layer thickness of $850 - 700 \; mb$ layer is $1592.36 \; m$
